## Overview

Fortexa is a security-operations product, so the interface should feel controlled, technical, and trustworthy before it feels expressive. The visual identity blends cloud-platform SaaS polish with banking-security restraint: compact controls, crisp borders, blue as the sole brand driver, and enough whitespace to make dense operational data readable.

The product has two complementary modes. Public and onboarding screens use a dramatic dark scanner-grid hero to establish the ATM/GAB security context. Authenticated dashboard screens become quieter and more utilitarian, with light cloud-gray page backgrounds, white panels, sticky navigation, dense tables, and status badges that let operators scan risk quickly.

## Colors

The palette is anchored by Fortexa Blue (`#0C5CAB`) and a bright operational blue (`#3B82F6`). Use blue for primary actions, active navigation, links, notification counts, chart series, and identity marks. Do not introduce additional brand accents for normal UI actions.

Light mode uses `#F8F9FA` as the page canvas, white cards, and low-contrast gray borders. Text is a deep blue-black rather than pure black. Dark mode uses near-black surfaces (`#09090B`, `#0F0F13`, `#141419`) with the same blue accent lifted to brighter values for legibility.

Semantic color is reserved for operational meaning: red for critical exposure and destructive feedback, orange for high severity, amber for at-risk or in-progress states, emerald for successful or mitigated states, and slate/gray for inactive metadata.

## Typography

IBM Plex Sans is the voice of the product. It reads as technical and institutional without becoming cold. Use bold IBM Plex Sans for page titles, hero statements, card section titles, and KPI values. Use regular or medium weights for body text and operational labels.

Large display type appears only in the login hero and high-value KPI numbers. Dashboard page titles should stay compact around 30px, section headings around 16px, and table/list content around 14px. Metadata, badges, breadcrumbs, table headers, and helper text live in the 10px-12px range.

IBM Plex Mono is only for IDs, asset codes, scanner references, and other machine-like identifiers. Do not use mono for general navigation or body copy.

## Layout

Use an 8px rhythm with 4px half-steps for tight component anatomy. Dashboard pages are built around a persistent 64px top bar, a 220px desktop sidebar, and a scrollable main area with 16px-24px page padding. Cards and chart panels usually sit in 20px or 24px gutters.

The preferred dashboard layout is a responsive grid with compact repeated cards above wider analysis panels and tables. Mobile layouts collapse to a single column, keeping the hero and form stacked vertically and preserving full-width controls.

Authenticated screens should feel like a work surface, not a marketing page. Use cards for meaningful containers such as KPIs, charts, forms, and tables. Avoid nested decorative cards; when grouping rows inside a card, prefer dividers, subtle row hover states, and white space.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth is subtle. Most hierarchy comes from tonal surfaces, 1px borders, sticky positioning, and compact shadows. Standard cards use a soft border and nearly flat shadow. Menus and dialogs use a stronger popover shadow but still keep a 1px ring.

Glass is used sparingly. The login hero and some panels use translucent dark surfaces over a scanner-grid background. Dashboard cards may use a mild glass-like treatment with high-opacity surfaces and blur, but never heavy frosted-glass decoration that interferes with data readability.

## Shapes

Fortexa uses rounded but disciplined geometry. Standard controls use 8px-10px corners, dashboard cards use 16px, and large application shells or hero panels may reach 24px. Badges, notification counters, avatars, search fields, and status dots are fully rounded.

Avoid mixing sharp and highly rounded forms in the same component group. The system should feel engineered and stable, not playful.

## Components

Primary buttons are solid Fortexa Blue with white text and a compact height. Use them once per decision area for the action that moves work forward. Hover states darken the blue; active states may press down by 1px.

Secondary and outline buttons use cloud-gray fills, gray text, and a blue-tinted hover. Icon-only buttons are square, 40px on touch-heavy surfaces, and turn blue on hover or focus.

Cards are white in light mode and charcoal in dark mode, with 16px corners and thin borders. KPI cards center their icon, value, label, and change indicator; the hero KPI may invert to a solid blue card.

Tables are dense and operational. Headers are uppercase, 12px, semibold, and gray. Rows use 12px vertical rhythm, subtle bottom borders, and light blue or gray hover states. Numeric and identifier-heavy cells may use tabular or mono styling.

Badges are small, rounded, and semibold. Severity badges must pair tinted backgrounds with strong text color. Status badges should include a small dot when communicating lifecycle state.

Forms use labels above inputs, helper/error text below, and 40px-44px input heights. Auth forms may use icon-leading inputs and segmented controls. Error and success messages should appear inline in tinted, rounded panels.

## Do's and Don'ts

- Do keep Fortexa Blue as the only normal interactive accent.
- Do use semantic severity colors only when the UI is describing risk, status, or validation.
- Do preserve dark-mode parity for every surface, border, and text role.
- Do keep dashboard typography compact so operators can scan rows, filters, and metrics quickly.
- Do use Lucide-style line icons with consistent stroke weight and restrained sizing.
- Don't use purple or multicolor gradients as a general brand treatment.
- Don't use pure black in product surfaces; use near-black charcoal values.
- Don't add heavy drop shadows to tables, cards, or navigation.
- Don't make dense dashboard pages feel like landing pages.
- Don't rely on color alone for severity or status; pair color with text labels, icons, dots, or ordering.
